My Programming Journey

I may not have taken the most conventional path to software development, but I believe "taking the path less traveled has made all the difference." I graduated from college five years ago and have gone through three career changes. When I first graduated, I worked as a Medical Research Technican. I loved the research, but hated the isolation.


My first career change came two years after I graduated from University. I loved science and research, but I needed to a way to give back to my community, while still incorporating my background in cognitive and developmental neuroscience. I decided to go into teaching--specifically infants and toddlers. Working so closely with children and their families was incredibly rewarding, and I know I have grown tramendously throughout my teaching career.


I am in the midst of my second career change. I love working with children, but my current position doesn't allow for a great deal of personal growth or innovation in the field. I have found that I think I would be happier in a career that allows for more creative freedom in project development.


“A jack of all trades is a master of none, but oftentimes better than a master of one.” I whole-heartedly identify with this quote because I feel my experiences show that I am versatile and adept at many things, and view myself as a multipotentialite. Multipotentialites thrive on learning, exploring, and mastering new skills. We are excellent at bringing disparate ideas together in creative ways. This makes us incredible innovators and problem solvers.
